## Strategic Insight: Why This Matters for Rootz

### CI targets investors. Steph trains agents.

This is a **different customer with overlapping data needs**:

| Dimension | CI (Investors) | Rootz (Listing Agents) |
|-----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Goal | Buy properties below market | Get listings to sell |
| Key metric | Discount to ARV | Listings per month |
| Signals needed | Distressed (pre-foreclosure, liens) | Voluntary (tired landlord, absentee) |
| Contact method | Cold call + text | Direct mail + door knock |
| Revenue model | Deal profit ($10K-$50K/flip) | Commission ($6K-$15K/listing) |
| Training focus | Negotiation, deal structure, funding | Farming methodology, market knowledge |
| AI need | Deal analysis, comps, ARV | "Why might this person sell?" |

### What Steph is doing wrong (and we can fix)

Steph is **using an investor tool to train listing agents**. CI is built for wholesalers who want to find distressed properties and flip them. Steph's 1,100 agents need:

1. **Farming territory analysis** — "show me the farming opportunity in ZIP 33076"
2. **Owner story** — "why might this person sell?" not just "is this property distressed?"
3. **Talking points** — "what do I say when I knock on this door?"
4. **Monthly farm reports** — automated neighborhood stats to mail to their farm
5. **Compliance guardrails** — FTSA auto-scrubbing, calling hour limits

None of this exists in CI. CI gives you a list and says "go call them."

### Our pitch to Steph

"Connected Investors is built for wholesalers who want to buy properties at a discount. Your agents don't want to BUY — they want to LIST. We built the farming tool that thinks like a listing agent, not an investor. It tells your agents WHO might sell, WHY they might sell, and WHAT to say when they reach out. And it costs a fraction of what CI charges."
